All stories have places, but all places also have stories. This project aims to connect stories to their places through an interactive map that highglights both Indigenous Land claims through Treaty maps, and Indigenous written literatures and/or books.

While we recognize that Indigenous relationships to the Land are both sacred and not constrained by the geographic boundaries that the numbered Treaties represent, we would like to suggest this map as a way to think about the relationships and backgrounds of Indigenous authors across the Land now called "Canada."

We respectfully acknowledge that we are creating this project in Amiskwaciwâskahikan, on Treaty 6 territory, a traditional gathering place for diverse Indigenous Peoples including the Cree, Blackfoot, Métis, Nakota Sioux, Iroquois, Dene, Ojibway/Saulteaux/Anishinaabe, Inuit, and many others whose histories, languages and cultures continue to influence our vibrant community.
